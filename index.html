What I Learned

In this lecture, I learn about the key parts and goals of the project proposals. So a project is a big task that needs careful planning, a lot of time, and effort. I learned two main types of proposals: External Proposals and Internal Proposals.

External Proposals mean proposals which seek to secure contracts by offering concise summaries, management specifics, and financial outlines.

Internal Proposals mean proposals which seek to secure contracts by offering concise summaries, management specifics, and financial outlines.

9. Telephone Etiquette

Attended

What I Learned

In this lecture, I learned about telephone etiquette and customer service. I learned how to answer the phone politely and introduce myself and my department. I learned to speak clearly and smile while talking. I also learned that I should return calls quickly and help the caller instead of just forwarding the call. I learned how to deal with angry callers in a calm and respectful way. This training helped me understand how to talk on the phone in a professional and helpful manner.

Short Notes

Telephone Etiquette and Customer Service – Key Points
• Answer Quickly – Pick up the phone within 3 rings.
• Identify Yourself – Say your name and department.
• Speak Politely – Use kind words and a friendly tone.
• Listen Carefully – Show interest and take notes.
• Take Messages Properly – Get names, numbers, and details clearly.
• Return Calls Fast – Call back within one working day.
• Handle Transfers Well – Tell the caller where you're sending them.
• Don't Use Slang – Speak clearly and professionally.
• Ask Before Holding – Always ask before putting someone on hold.
• Help Angry Callers – Stay calm, listen, and be respectful.
• Go the Extra Mile – Do more than expected to help.
• Use People's Names – It makes communication friendlier.
• Always Say Goodbye – End the call politely and clearly.
